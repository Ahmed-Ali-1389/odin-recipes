Fried Cauliflower

Description

Egyptian Fried Cauliflower offers a tantalizing twist on a beloved vegetable, transforming it into a flavorful delicacy. Fresh cauliflower florets are dipped in a seasoned batter, typically made with flour, spices, and sometimes a hint of garlic or herbs, before being fried to crispy perfection. The result is a crunchy exterior that gives way to a tender and succulent center, bursting with savory goodness.

Served hot and golden brown, Egyptian Fried Cauliflower is often accompanied by a zesty tahini sauce or a squeeze of lemon juice, enhancing its already delightful taste. This dish is cherished for its irresistible crunch, delightful flavor, and versatility, making it a cherished part of Egyptian cuisine enjoyed by all.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic/li>
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • salt to taste
  • Â¼ cup milk, or as needed
  • 2 cups vegetable oil, or as needed

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil; add 2 teaspoons cumin to boiling water. Cook cauliflower florets, working in batches, in boiling water until cooked but still crisp, 1 to 2 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and cool on a plate.
  2. Blend flour, egg, garlic, 1 teaspoon cumin, tomato paste, and salt in a blender or food processor until batter is smooth; thin with milk as needed.
  3. Heat oil in a deep skillet to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. Dip cauliflower florets in batter; fry battered cauliflower in hot oil, working in batches, until cauliflower is golden and crispy, 6 to 8 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
